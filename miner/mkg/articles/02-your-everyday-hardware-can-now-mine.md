# Your Everyday Hardware Can Now Mine Real Rewards

One of the most common reactions when people hear about crypto mining is: "That's only for big companies with warehouses full of machines, right?"

Bitcoin proved that decentralized mining works at global scale — a remarkable achievement. But the economics of proof-of-work hash mining naturally favor massive operations with cheap power and thousands of specialized ASICs, which over time concentrates participation among the largest players.

The PoH Miner network takes the same proven decentralized model and applies it to a different kind of work — running AI inference on every participating device — which changes who can meaningfully participate.

You don't need a giant facility. You don't even need the most powerful hardware on the market. In many cases, your existing gaming PC, a Mac Mini, a decent desktop, or even a well-specced laptop can participate meaningfully — and earn real POH rewards for doing useful work.

## Why Small and Medium Hardware Actually Has an Advantage Here

In Bitcoin's hash-rate mining, raw computational power is the dominant factor — more machines mean a higher chance of winning the block. That model makes perfect sense for securing a ledger through proof of work.

In the PoH Miner network, two factors matter enormously:

1. **Speed and latency** — The first valid, high-quality result wins the job. Being physically or network-close to where requests originate gives you a real edge.
2. **Quality of work** — You have to actually run the real POH AI checker across most of the live signals. Lazy or incomplete results get rejected, and repeat offenders face reputation penalties.

This changes the game completely.

A well-optimized machine in Europe or North America can win jobs against much more powerful hardware on the other side of the planet because the results arrive faster. A gamer's desktop that stays online and delivers consistent, high-quality analyses can outperform a poorly maintained "mining farm" that cuts corners.

Geography becomes an asset instead of irrelevant.

## What Kind of Hardware Actually Works?

The PoH Miner software is built to run on commodity hardware:

- **Gaming PCs** (especially those with decent CPUs and some GPU headroom) perform very well.
- **Mac Minis** (M1, M2, M3, M4) are excellent — quiet, efficient, reliable, and great for always-on operation.
- **Modern desktops** with 8+ cores and 16GB+ RAM can participate comfortably.
- **Higher-end laptops** work when plugged in and not thermal-throttling.
- Even some **mini PCs** and well-equipped single-board computers can contribute on lighter workloads.

The software supports CPU, GPU (via compatible inference backends), and automatic modes. It doesn't require exotic setups.

You also get the POH Miner Wallet (Android) so you can track your earnings, receive POH, and send it — all without needing to run a full node yourself if you don't want to.

## The Economics Are More Approachable Too

Every block pays a fixed 1 POH reward (split between the block proposer and the workers who delivered results for the jobs in that block).

There are no crazy difficulty swings that suddenly make small participants irrelevant. The network wants broad geographic distribution because that actually improves the quality and speed of the verification service.

This means:

- Consistent participants with good uptime are rewarded.
- Location and reliability matter as much as peak performance.
- You can start small, learn the system, and scale up over time (add more machines, optimize your setup, run in multiple regions).

It's much closer to "running a useful service that earns money" — where location, uptime, and quality of inference matter as much as raw hardware.

## Not Just for Hardcore Miners

One of the most exciting things about this design is who it opens the door for.

- A student with a good gaming rig.
- A small business owner who already has always-on servers.
- Someone who wants to put an old but capable Mac Mini to work instead of letting it collect dust.
- People who care about the mission (keeping the internet human) and want their hardware contributing to AI-era verification work.

You still need to understand the basics — keep the software updated, monitor quality scores, make sure you're running the real signal checks — but the barrier is accessible to anyone with commodity hardware, not just industrial-scale operations.

## The Bigger Picture

When mining becomes accessible to normal hardware owners instead of only the largest players, something interesting happens to the network:

It becomes more decentralized in practice, not just in theory.

It becomes more resilient.

And it becomes something regular people can feel good about running — because the work their machine is doing is directly solving one of the most pressing problems of the AI era: keeping the internet honest about who is human.

Your everyday hardware is no longer "not powerful enough."

In this network, it might be exactly the right tool for the job.
