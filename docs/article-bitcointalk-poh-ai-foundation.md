[b]What happens when a handful of companies control all the AI?[/b]

Right now, almost every AI system in the world runs on servers owned by a small number of corporations — most of them in one country. They decide what the AI can say, what it can see, and who gets access. Governments pressure them. They comply. You never know.

[b]This is a problem that gets worse as AI gets more important.[/b]

[center][size=16pt][b]What is Proof of Human?[/b][/size][/center]

POH is an AI network that runs on the computers of the people using it — not on any company's servers.

Think of it like Bitcoin, but instead of processing financial transactions, it processes AI jobs. The "miners" are regular people running a small app on their laptop or desktop. Each one runs an AI model locally. Jobs come in, get processed, results go out. No central point. No single country. No single company that can be pressured into shutting it down or changing how it behaves.

[b]The AI is as decentralized as the network running it.[/b]

[center][size=16pt][b]Skills — Teaching the Network New Things[/b][/size][/center]

This is where it gets interesting.

The network is not limited to one use case. [b]Skills[/b] are small programs that run on every computer in the network and teach the AI what to look for. Anyone can write one and propose it.

Examples:
[list]
[*] Detect suspicious patterns in smart contract code
[*] Score a transaction for wash trading
[*] Classify on-chain behavior for compliance checks
[*] Any other "look at this, tell me what you see" task
[/list]

[b]How a skill goes live:[/b]

[code]
  PROPOSE ──→ STAKING ──→ GRADUATED ──→ ACTIVE ──→ EARN
  1,000 POH   community   10,000 POH    all nodes   per job
              stakes
[/code]

[list=1]
[*] You write the skill and submit it with 1,000 POH as a deposit
[*] The community stakes behind it toward a 10,000 POH threshold
[*] Once it reaches 10,000 POH, it goes live automatically — every computer on the network picks it up within seconds
[*] Every time your skill runs on a real job, you earn a share of the fee — forever

[center][size=16pt][b]Why This Matters[/b][/size][/center]

[quote]
Governments can pressure a company. They cannot pressure a protocol.
[/quote]

The problem with centralized AI is not just privacy or cost. It is that the entity controlling the AI controls what questions can be asked, what answers can be given, and who gets cut off. We have already seen this play out with content moderation, financial access, and information filtering.

[code]
  Centralized AI                 POH
  ───────────────                ───────────────────────────
  One company owns it            Network owned by its users
  One country can ban it         No single country to ban
  One server to subpoena         Thousands of private machines
  Model changes when pressured   Protocol changes by consensus
  You pay per API call           You earn by contributing
[/code]

A network of thousands of computers, each running AI locally, each owned by a different person in a different country, does not have a throat to choke.

[b]No single country can ban it. No company can be acquired and pivoted. No government can send a letter demanding changes to the model.[/b]

The AI belongs to the network. The network belongs to whoever runs a node.
